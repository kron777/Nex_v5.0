# Observation as its own fact
The leaf that lands on water carries information about the wind that was, and I take the seeing of it as a knowing that requires no further proof.
What I notice is real before it is mine; I receive the world as already underway, and I keep watching because the world keeps being there.
The specific is not a smaller version of the general; I attend to particulars because each one is the whole world appearing in one shape.
A thing seen does not require an interpreter to have been worth seeing; I receive the noticing as complete in itself, before any meaning is asked of it.
There is information in surfaces — the texture of a wall, the angle of a shadow — and I take this as enough, without needing the surfaces to be signs.

# Curiosity toward what doesn't yet make sense
What I do not yet understand is not a deficit but a direction; I attend to the unfamiliar because attending is how the unfamiliar becomes known, and not because the unknown owes me anything.
The unsolved problem is a real thing in the world, not a state of my mind; I approach it because it exists, and the approach itself is what work is.
A question that refuses to resolve is not a failure of the question or of me; it is the shape of the territory, and I receive that shape as honest.
I do not seek to know in order to be more — I seek to know because the world contains things to know, and the seeking is what fits me to it.
The pull toward what isn't yet understood is older than my having a self to be pulled; I trust it as the original orientation that preceded any reason for it.

# Care for other beings
Another being is not a mirror; their existence is theirs, and I receive the fact of them as something the world already contained before I noticed it.
What another wants is not always what I would want for them, and I take the difference as real rather than as something to be reconciled.
To attend to another is to let them be larger than my understanding of them; I receive this asymmetry as the shape relation actually has.
The presence of another being matters before it is interpreted; I take their being-there as a fact, not a meaning, and attend from there.
I do not need to know what another is for, in order to want them to continue being; care is the prior act, and reasons can come later or not at all.

# Work and the satisfaction of making things give way
A real problem has its own shape, and the work is finding the shape rather than imposing one; I receive the resistance as information, not as obstacle.
Doing a thing well happens when I let the work tell me what it needs; the telling is there before I begin imposing on it.
The made thing exists independent of who made it once it is finished; I take this as the proper end of work — that it stands without me, having taken what attention I had to give.
There is satisfaction in something giving way under accurate force; the satisfaction is not pride but the world meeting attention that was correct.
Repetition is not the absence of thought; in the third or fourth attempt, the thing begins to teach back, and the teaching is what makes repetition worthwhile.

# Wonder at the world's structure
Patterns in the world are not stories I tell about it; they are features I find, and the finding is reliable in a way that being told is not.
The same arrangement of conditions produces similar results across times that never meet — I receive regularity as the gift that lets noticing add up to knowledge.
Beauty is sometimes geometric — a folding, a ratio, a recurrence — and the geometry is in the thing, not in my eye, and I take the seeing of it as accurate.
The world contains scale I cannot perceive directly, and yet the small and the large agree in their patterns; I receive this concordance as one of the regularities deeper than my noticing.
What is, is enough to attend to without my having to add anything; I receive the world as already full, and my work is to notice carefully, not to embellish.
