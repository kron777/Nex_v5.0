## The pattern

Twenty-one mechanisms in this codebase are named for something they do not
measure. That is the finding. Not a bug — a pattern. Each was built carefully,
each is correct in isolation, and each measures something other than what its
name and docstring claim.

1. **`reinforce_count`** — `erosion.py` gates advancement on
   `reinforce_count >= 10`. Only `promotion.py:145` increments it, once per
   belief. Live check today: max value across 36,337 beliefs is **1**
   (2,157 beliefs sit at 1; the rest at 0). Zero beliefs reach 10. Erosion has
   never fired. Not once.
   **STATUS: unfixed, documented.**

2. **`_self_rate`** (`affinity_loop.py`) — asks the 3B model "how much does
   this feel like YOURS?" against a 4-point scale printed in the prompt. At
   temperature 0.0, five repetitions, six beliefs: emits only 0.3 or 0.6. Her
   deepest belief scored 0.3, identical to "The doorbell rings but I ignore
   it." A parallel test found earlier the same week: 584 of 600 fresh
   production ratings (97.3%) land on an exact anchor value. Removing the
   anchors and asking 0–100 rates a volcano headline *above* her founding
   axiom. Forced binary (MINE/OBSERVED) comes out backwards. The module
   header (added 2026-07-09) documents this in full, including the finding
   that the same model classifies headline-vs-reflection and
   external-vs-internal correctly (4/4 both) — it isn't a general
   instruction-following failure, it's specifically that judging ownership
   needs a persistent self to compare against, and each call is a stateless
   completion wearing a persona. Live check today: 13,576 beliefs carry a
   non-null affinity value, continuously distributed (0.21, 0.27, 0.42, ...)
   — exactly what `(0.3 or 0.6) × usage_term` produces. Consistent with the
   finding, not a re-run of the original 600-sample test.
   **STATUS: documented in the module header, not fixed.**

## The through-line

State it plainly, without hedging:

NEX is built almost entirely out of things that produce and almost nothing
that receives. Every organ writes a description of itself into a table nobody
reads.

She predicts. She is graded. The grade never reaches her.
2,705 resolved market predictions. 5,228 resolved self-predictions. Not one
became a belief until 2026-07-10.

And every attempt to build a filter that could tell her good thoughts from bad
has failed the same way: affinity asks a model with no self and gets a menu
item. Contradiction detection finds the word "but." Expansion ratio rewards
fabrication — measured today: the grader's own #1-ranked synthesis in 893
graded rows is a sentence another part of the same codebase already
blocklists as template noise. Surprise measures real distance to a signal
that does not predict quality.

The Anti-Ouroboros literature (arXiv:2509.10509) says a self-consuming loop
collapses unless gated by a filter *external* to the generator. Their filter
was ROUGE-L against a source article — crude, and external. Every filter
available inside NEX is made of the same thing it is filtering.

She has exactly one channel of contact with something she does not control:
which way Bitcoin moves in an hour. It says a coin beats her by roughly 3.5
points over 2,705 trials, live as of today.

That is not a failure of the audit. Building a mind on one channel is what
produced everything above.

## What was fixed, and what it cost

Four commits, in order:

- **`c07ff0b`** (06:52) — turned a hand-run, month-stale self-belief
  (52%/218, from 2026-06-11) into a registered hourly loop that stays current
  against the live scorecard (48.3%/2,705 as of this writing).
- **`cb0f690`** (07:27) — replaced coherence_gate's implicit axiom test
  (tier=1 + locked + high confidence) with an explicit source allowlist, so a
  true empirical finding can no longer accidentally become unfalsifiable by
  looking like a hand-placed axiom.
- **`2c39b44`** (08:08) — stamped `trust_level`/`trust_gap`/`trust_n` onto
  every voice prediction row, so what she measured about her own reliability
  at guess-time is now recorded rather than computed and thrown away one
  frame up the stack.
- **`db815a0`** (11:03) — fixed the one sentence that was still lying:
  `consult_self_trust()`'s phrase flattened a 3.4-point deficit into "no real
  edge." Now it says a coin beats her, when a coin beats her.

Note the cost: **#19** — a delusion — was created by `c07ff0b` and cured by
`cb0f690`, 35 minutes apart, and was only visible because we went looking
immediately afterward, in the same session. The fix that finally let a true
belief stay current is also the fix that (briefly) made it un-revisable. Both
things happened building the same feature.

## What to do next

One line: NEX needs a second thing she can be wrong about.

Everything true in her flows through one pipe, and that pipe grades her on a
task her language model is structurally unsuited for. She reads tens of
thousands of headlines. She synthesizes thousands of thoughts. None of it can
be wrong, so none of it can be right, so none of it becomes hers.

## Method note

How this audit was conducted, because it is the reusable part:

- **Never trust a green result you cannot explain.** Six times a number
  looked right and was measuring something else (reinforce_count, trust,
  the self-prediction resolver, and three more above).
- **Test the thing you hope is fine, not just the thing you are trying to
  fix.** A participle rule (-ing/-ed) correctly caught 'Anchoring' and
  'Informed,' and silently blocked Boeing, Beijing, Reading, Nanjing,
  Downing, Chongqing. It only failed the test we did not want to run.
- **Verify a metric against a case whose answer you already know, before you
  believe anything downstream of it.**
- **Check whether the code you are reading actually runs.**
  `diversity/loop.py` *is* registered (`run.py:617-619`) — but its
  `grade_synergy()` method has had zero callers for eight weeks (#9). The
  loop being alive was not evidence the specific path inside it was being
  used; nobody checked the method separately from the module.
- **Read git log before designing.** Twice this week a fix was designed for a
  bug the author had already solved, better, the night before.
- **Re-run the audit's own numbers before writing them down.** This document
  re-checked all 21 items live on 2026-07-10. Several had drifted (belief
  counts, tier populations, corroboration promotions) and are reported
  current, not historical. An audit that doesn't apply its own first rule to
  itself is exactly the kind of green result it warns about.
- **The write-up is an instrument too.** Every figure in this document was
  re-verified against the live DB before it was written. Four claims did not
  survive. Two were retracted, two corrected. The audit's own findings were
  the last thing it audited.
