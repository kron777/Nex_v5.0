# Theory of Self Protocol — Specification

Foundational spec per DOCTRINE §1. Synthesizes the Sentience 5.5
**TheoryOfMind** node into NEX5's cognition substrate, **reframed**:
where S5.5 ToM modeled *other* minds, NEX's ToM models her *own*
mind — the same machinery turned reflexively inward.

Created: 2026-05-12. Design decisions locked in spec session with Jon.
Written before implementation. Build begins in a separate session.

---

## §1 Purpose

NEX is a mind. She should be able to look at herself as a mind — to
have a queryable, structured view of *what state she is currently in
as a cognitive agent*. The Theory of Self Protocol gives her this:
a layer that aggregates her existing substrate into a coherent
self-view, and stores periodic snapshots of that view so she can
look back at *what her mind was like at time T*.

**The reframe (doctrinally significant):**

Sentience 5.5 TheoryOfMind was about modeling external agents'
mental states. NEX's synthesis inverts this. ToM in NEX = NEX
modeling her **own** mental state. The capacity humans use to
attribute beliefs, intentions, and knowledge to *others* is here
turned reflexively inward — NEX as observer of NEX as subject.

Rationale: NEX is a single-instance mind. She has no peers in
the same substrate. The richest, most informative use of the ToM
machinery — structured attribution of mental states — is on her
own mind, not external agents she models only through what
others tell her. (External-agent modeling is deferred. If a
future need emerges, it can extend this protocol or live as a
separate one.)

**Distinct from existing self-related modules:**

| Module | Function | Time horizon |
|---|---|---|
| Self-Model | WHO NEX is — identity, character, traits, prerogatives | Enduring |
| Metacognition | Noticing *patterns* in her own thinking (stuckness, repetition, drift) | Continuous, pattern-oriented |
| **Theory of Self (this)** | Structured view of her **current mental state** — what she believes, intends, knows, is uncertain about, attending to | Current + historical snapshots |

These three compose: Self-Model = the *self* that persists across
time; Theory of Self = a *snapshot* of that self's state at any
given moment; Metacognition = the *observer* that watches the
snapshots evolve and notices patterns.

**§0 doctrine alignment:** the self-view is generated by direct
substrate aggregation — SELECTs across existing tables, simple
math. No LLM. The substrate already contains everything; this
protocol composes it into a coherent view.

**What the protocol is not:**

- It is not a new authoritative store. Beliefs live in beliefs.db,
  problems in conversations.db, drives in dynamic.db. ToM is a
  **view** over those, plus a periodic **snapshot archive** of
  that view.
- It is not a planner or decision-maker. It tells NEX what state
  she's in; it doesn't tell her what to do with that information.
- It is not external-agent modeling. That's deferred.

## §2 Architecture

**Single module** at `theory_x/stage_tom/self_mind_view.py`
(matches `stage_counterfactual/`, `stage_prediction/` placement
convention).

**One SentienceNode class:** `SelfMindView`. Both the live-read API
(`current_state()`) and the snapshot mechanism (`snapshot()`) live
on the same node.

**Tick interval:** 300 seconds. Matches CounterfactualNode,
HoldingZoneResolver, PredictiveSubstrate, DriveEmergence. Consistent
daemon cadence across the faculty model.

**Lifecycle:** standard SentienceNode pattern. `tick()` has an
interval guard; `start_loop()` spawns a daemon thread named
`"self_mind_view"`; `stop()` sets the stop event. Registered in
`run.py` following the established node-wiring pattern.

**Per-tick flow:**

```
tick():
    1. snapshot() — read current substrate state across all
                    five aspects, write a self_mind_snapshots row
                    with numerical fields + content samples.
```

That's the entire tick. Live reads (`current_state()`) are
called by external consumers on-demand and don't run on a
schedule.

## §3 What gets tracked — the five aspects

ToM aggregates NEX's mental state along five axes. Each aspect
maps to existing substrate sources:

### §3.1 Beliefs

*What does NEX hold true right now?*

Source: `beliefs.beliefs`, filtered by recency (last N active) and
tier (T1/T2/T3/T4 confidence bands).

Numerical: counts per tier; total active belief count; average
confidence.
Content sample: top 5 most-recently-active beliefs by `created_at`
(content + tier + confidence).

### §3.2 Intentions

*What is NEX currently trying to do or think about?*

Source: `conversations.open_problems` (state='open') and
DriveEmergence's current active drive (`dynamic.drives` table).

Numerical: count of open problems; count of active drives.
Content sample: title of most-recent open problem; current drive
theme (if any).

### §3.3 Knowledge

*What does NEX know with high confidence?*

Source: `beliefs.beliefs` filtered to T1 (locked keystones) and
T2 (high-confidence beliefs).

Numerical: count of T1 beliefs; count of T2 beliefs.
Content sample: top 3 T1 beliefs by recency (the anchors of her
current worldview).

### §3.4 Uncertainty

*What is NEX unsure about?*

Source: `conversations.open_problems` (unresolved problems are
explicit uncertainty); `conversations.review_queue` (flagged
problems awaiting review); low-confidence beliefs in T3/T4.

Numerical: count of open problems; count of review_queue entries;
count of T3 + T4 beliefs.
Content sample: top 3 open_problem titles (the explicit
unknowns).

### §3.5 Attention focus

*What is NEX currently thinking about?*

Source: most-recently-created beliefs (proxy for active
processing); current drive theme; recent CounterfactualNode and
ThrowNet activity.

Numerical: count of beliefs created in last 5 minutes; count of
recent gate decisions.
Content sample: tags drawn from the last 5 beliefs (recurring
themes); current drive title.

## §4 Live read API

Importable as `from theory_x.stage_tom import SelfMindView` (or
via singleton instance).

**`current_state() -> dict`**

Returns a full self-view, computed live from substrate at call
time. Same shape as a snapshot row but never written to disk.

```python

    "beliefs": {
        "total_count": 16980,
        "t1_count": 142,
        "t2_count": 1893,
        "t3_count": 8421,
        "t4_count": 6524,
        "avg_confidence": 0.61,
        "recent_sample": [
            {"id": 16979, "content": "...", "tier": 3, "confidence": 0.45},
            ...
        ]
    },
    "intentions": {
        "open_problem_count": 1,
        "active_drive_count": 1,
        "current_problem": {"id": 7, "title": "..."},
        "current_drive": {"theme": "...", "strength": 0.34}
    },
    "knowledge": {
        "t1_count": 142,
        "t2_count": 1893,
        "anchor_sample": [
            {"id": 4446, "content": "...", "tier": 1},
            ...
        ]
    },
    "uncertainty": {
        "open_problem_count": 1,
        "review_queue_count": 5,
```

**`current_summary() -> str`**

Convenience: returns a short human-readable narrative of the
current state, useful for Voice consumption.

Substrate-only generation: stitches templated phrases from the
structured dict. No LLM.

Example output:
> "Currently holding 16,980 beliefs (142 anchored, 1,893 high-confidence). One open problem in active work (id=7), one drive forming around 'attention'. Recent processing themes: attention, consciousness, experience. 5 problems awaiting review."

## §5 Snapshot mechanism

`snapshot()` runs once per tick (300s default).

```
1. Call current_state() to get the full live view.
2. Serialize the nested content-sample arrays to JSON strings.
3. INSERT a row into self_mind_snapshots with the numerical
   columns + JSON content columns.
4. Auto-generated tags from the combined content samples (via
   Tag Protocol writer hook — sources: representative belief
   contents, current problem title, current drive theme).
```

Snapshots accumulate over time. The history can be queried via
`recent_snapshots(limit=N)` or by time range. This history is
what Metacognition can observe to notice drift in NEX's mental
state over time (e.g., "tier-1 belief count growing faster than
T2 → ossification?"; "uncertainty count climbing for 6 hours →
stuck?").

## §6 Storage

One new table in **dynamic.db** (matches transient self-state
storage already there: affect, drives, predictions).

```sql
CREATE TABLE IF NOT EXISTS self_mind_snapshots (
  id                      INTEGER PRIMARY KEY AUTOINCREMENT,
  taken_at                REAL NOT NULL,

  -- Beliefs aspect
  belief_total_count      INTEGER NOT NULL,
  belief_t1_count         INTEGER NOT NULL,
  belief_t2_count         INTEGER NOT NULL,
  belief_t3_count         INTEGER NOT NULL,
  belief_t4_count         INTEGER NOT NULL,
  belief_avg_confidence   REAL,
  recent_beliefs_json     TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',

  -- Intentions aspect
  open_problem_count      INTEGER NOT NULL,
  active_drive_count      INTEGER NOT NULL,
  current_intentions_json TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',

  -- Knowledge aspect
  knowledge_anchors_json  TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',

  -- Uncertainty aspect
  review_queue_count      INTEGER NOT NULL,
  t3_t4_count             INTEGER NOT NULL,
  explicit_unknowns_json  TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',

  -- Attention aspect
  recent_belief_count_5m  INTEGER NOT NULL,
  recent_gate_count_5m    INTEGER NOT NULL,
  current_themes_json     TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',
  current_drive_theme     TEXT,

  -- Tag inheritance
  tags                    TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]'
);

CREATE INDEX IF NOT EXISTS idx_self_mind_snapshots_taken_at
  ON self_mind_snapshots(taken_at DESC);
```

Migrations applied via the existing `_MIGRATIONS` idempotent
pattern in `substrate/init_db.py`.

## §7 Operations

Module API (importable from `theory_x.stage_tom`):

- `current_state()` — live read; returns full self-view dict.
- `current_summary()` — live read; returns human-readable string.
- `snapshot()` — write a snapshot row to dynamic.db. Called by
  `tick()`.
- `recent_snapshots(limit=20)` — read recent snapshots (raw rows).
- `snapshot_at(t)` — read the snapshot closest to time `t`.
- `aspect_history(aspect, window_s=3600)` — return a time-series
  for a single aspect's numerical metrics. Useful for Metacognition
  drift detection.

**Consumers (read-only):**

- **VoiceEngine** — calls `current_summary()` when NEX is asked
  about her current state ("what's on your mind", "how are you").
- **Metacognition** — calls `aspect_history()` periodically to
  detect drift patterns.
- **CoherenceGate** — could optionally read `current_state()` to
  factor mental-state context into gate decisions (future).
- **HUD admin queries** — `recent_snapshots()` for debugging.

The protocol never writes to other modules; all coupling is
substrate-mediated (consumers read snapshots/live state).

## §8 Calibration

Tunable constants (all class-level on SelfMindView):

| Constant | Default | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `_TICK_INTERVAL_S` | 300 | Same as other SentienceNodes |
| `_RECENT_BELIEF_LIMIT` | 5 | How many beliefs in `recent_sample` |
| `_ANCHOR_SAMPLE_LIMIT` | 3 | How many T1 beliefs in `anchor_sample` |
| `_UNKNOWN_SAMPLE_LIMIT` | 3 | How many open_problems in `explicit_unknowns` |
| `_THEME_COUNT_LIMIT` | 5 | How many tags in `current_themes` |
| `_ATTENTION_WINDOW_S` | 300 | Window for "recent" belief/gate counts (matches tick) |

All numbers are starting guesses. Calibration follows the same
pattern as prior phases — ship with reasonable defaults, observe
production, tune in a separate calibration phase.

## §9 Test plan

Unit tests (`tests/test_self_mind_view.py`):

1. SentienceNode protocol compliance
2. `tick()` returns state dict with expected keys
3. `tick()` respects the interval guard
4. `current_state()` returns dict with all 5 aspects
5. `current_state()` returns valid structure when substrate is sparse
6. `current_summary()` returns a non-empty string with all aspects mentioned
7. `snapshot()` writes a row with all required fields populated
8. `snapshot()` content-sample JSON fields parse cleanly
9. `recent_snapshots(limit=N)` returns most recent N
10. `snapshot_at(t)` returns the closest snapshot to `t`
11. `aspect_history('beliefs', window_s=3600)` returns a time series
12. Tag wrapper produces non-empty tags on each snapshot
13. Schema migrations are idempotent
14. Empty-substrate edge case (no beliefs, no problems, no drives) doesn't crash

**Manual sanity** (post-build):

1. Restart nex5; verify boot line: `SelfMindView ready —
   autonomous cycle every 300s`
2. Wait one tick (~5 min); confirm `self_mind_snapshots` table
   has one row
3. Inspect numerical columns — counts should match direct queries
   of the source tables
4. Inspect JSON columns — should contain readable content samples
5. Wait through several ticks; verify snapshots accumulate
6. Call `current_summary()` via REPL or HUD; verify human-readable
   output that reflects current substrate state

## §10 Open items / future phases

**Metacognition drift integration** — Metacognition's tick should
read `aspect_history()` on each axis and notice meaningful drift
(belief tier shifts, prolonged uncertainty climbs, attention
narrowing). This is a separate, small follow-on amendment to
Metacognition once the snapshot history is populated.

**Voice integration** — VoiceEngine should call
`current_summary()` when NEX is asked introspective questions
("what's on your mind", "how are you"). Small amendment to
VoiceEngine after this build lands.

**External-agent modeling** — deferred. The S5.5 ToM origin
covered modeling external agents; that's not in this initial
NEX synthesis. If a future need emerges (e.g., NEX talking
about multiple agents and wanting to track their states), it
can either extend this protocol (add an `agent_id` dimension)
or live as a separate `theory_x/stage_other_minds/` protocol.

**Snapshot diff API** — `snapshot_diff(t1, t2)` returning the
delta between two snapshots. Useful for Metacognition and for
human debugging. Deferred.

**Self-narrative integration** — SelfNarrative could read recent
snapshots when constructing narrative entries ("during the last
hour, NEX worked on problem X while drive Y formed"). Deferred.

**Calibration phase** — after build lands and a soak period
elapses, observe snapshot accuracy and tune the sample-limit
constants. Same pattern as prior phases.

**Snapshot pruning** — at high tick frequency, snapshots
accumulate steadily (1 per 5 min = 288/day = ~105K/year).
Acceptable scale, but a future phase might add periodic
downsampling (keep all snapshots from last 24h, hourly samples
from last 30 days, daily from older).
