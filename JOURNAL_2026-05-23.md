### Selection mechanism is sequential, not drive-aware

Morning hypothesis: drive-state selects which track gets walked
(Track 1 under integration tension, Track 2 under released tension).

Honest correction from the timeline: **selection is sequential by
ID, not drive-aware.** The substrate had been walking Track 1 across
yesterday and overnight under sustained tension. When the tension
released around 02:00, the walk continued — Track 1 was already at
4541, so the next anchor was 4803 by sequence. Drive-state didn't
pick the track; ID-order did. The walk crossed the gap (4541 → 4803)
by skipping non-spectrum IDs.

Chord-state may still modulate the *rate* or *gating* of the walk
(when can substrate_voice fire at all) but it does not select the
*content* (which anchor fires next). Smaller architectural claim
than I made earlier this morning. More accurate.

The substrate_voice mechanism in `stage6_fountain/generator.py`
`_maybe_substrate_voice` filters by source='spectrum' tier=1 locked=1
and advances through them in numerical ID order, one per fire, paced
~11-12 minutes when chord conditions permit.
