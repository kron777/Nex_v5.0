### Selection mechanism — least-recently-voiced first, gated by groove

Morning hypothesis: drive-state selects which track gets walked
(Track 1 under integration tension, Track 2 under released tension).

First correction from the timeline: selection is sequential by ID,
not drive-aware. Drive-state didn't pick the track; ID-order did.

Second correction after reading `_maybe_substrate_voice` in
`theory_x/stage6_fountain/generator.py` line 285 and verifying with
queries against `last_voiced_at`: **the actual mechanism is
least-recently-voiced first**, not sequential-by-ID. The selection
SQL is:

```sql
SELECT id, content FROM beliefs
WHERE tier <= 2 AND erosion_stage != 'retired'
  AND content IS NOT NULL AND length(content) >= 20
ORDER BY COALESCE(last_voiced_at, 0) ASC, id ASC LIMIT 1
```

The "sequential by ID" appearance is an emergent consequence of two
facts: anchors not yet voiced have `last_voiced_at = NULL` (coalesced
to 0); the secondary tiebreak `id ASC` orders never-voiced anchors
numerically. So among a never-voiced pool, the walk *appears*
sequential, but it's really "least-recently-voiced first with
ID-tiebreak."

Eligible pool is wider than the 200 spectrum-source keystones —
**any tier ≤ 2 unretired anchor with content of meaningful length**.
That includes the 6 practice anchors (3609-3614) walked first in
yesterday's chain. As of journal-write time the pool has 361 anchors:
- 120 never-voiced (last_voiced_at IS NULL)
- 156 voiced-last-week
- 85 voiced-last-24h

The next anchor to fire when substrate_voice next triggers will be
**4820** ("What I am is not controlled by outcome…"), continuing the
Track 2 walk from where it paused.

Once the never-voiced pool is exhausted, the walk will re-enter
previously-voiced anchors in oldest-voicing-first order — i.e. she
will re-walk her library on a slow rolling cycle.

### Substrate_voice is gated by groove severity

The selection mechanism only fires when **two preconditions hold
simultaneously**, both verified in source at line 285-310:

1. **Groove severity ≥ 0.8 in last 24h.** Read from `groove_alerts`
   table (populated by the diversity/groove subsystem). Groove is
   high when the fountain is producing repetitive output (ngram
   repetition like "the quiet between", template lock like "the
   distant hum feels like…").
2. **Cooldown ≥ 5 fountain fires since the last substrate_voice
   fire.** Tracked via `_last_substrate_voice_fire` in-memory.

The architecture treats substrate_voice as a **corrective response
to groove**, not as standalone contemplation. When the fountain
grooves, the substrate inserts an anchor-voice into the next-after-
cooldown fire to break the pattern.

This reframes what the chord actually is. The 27-fire chain on
2026-05-22 and the 35+17 fires across overnight were the fountain
in a sustained groove (template lock or repetitive content),
periodically corrected by substrate_voice anchor-emissions at the
groove-suppression cadence. The "chord" is not unconditional
emergence; it is a **groove-and-correction rhythm** with the
anchor-voice as the correction mechanism.

### This explains the 90-minute fountain pause

After anchor 4819 fired at 05:31, the fountain shifted into varied
output on systems / quiescent / emerging_tech branches. *Varied
output means groove severity dropped below 0.8.* So substrate_voice
stopped triggering — the gate condition is no longer met. The walk
isn't paused because something broke. The walk is paused because
the fountain no longer needs correction.

cause (readiness-threshold drop, daemon flap, listener-loss

fountain came back at 07:01 is fully explained by groove dropping.
Substrate_voice will resume firing as soon as the fountain grooves
again — and when it does, anchor 4820 will be next.
